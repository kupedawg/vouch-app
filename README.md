# Vouch — Community Trust Protocol

> Trust built by community. Verified by those who showed up before anyone was watching.

## What is Vouch?

Vouch is an open source community trust protocol. It lets registered charities vouch for trusted people and organisations — creating a public, permanent, human record of who has shown up for their community.

This is not a rating system. There are no stars, no comments, no downvotes. Only verified charities can write anything. Silence is the negative signal — if nobody has vouched for you, that says everything without saying anything.

## Why charities?

Registered charities already hold a trust credential issued by the state. They earned it by serving communities before anyone was watching. They never had a profit motive. Governments and businesses pull out — charities stay.

In Aotearoa New Zealand, that credential is a Charities Services registration number. In Australia it is ACNC. In the UK it is the Charity Commission. In the US it is 501(c)(3). Every country has a version. That registration is the root node. Everything flows from there.

## The problem it solves

In 2026 trust in media, institutions and governments is being systematically undermined. AI is going to sit over that layer and target our decisions. The question becomes — who do you trust?

Vouch answers that by letting communities speak for each other in a way that cannot be faked, bought, or gamed.

## How a vouch works

A charity coordinator logs in with their verified registration number. They answer three questions about the person or organisation they are vouching for:

1. What did this person do for your community?
2. Who specifically benefited?
3. Would you stake your organisation's reputation on them?

That vouch is timestamped, public, and permanent. The charity's name, registration number, and years of service sit beside every word they write.

## Design principles

- Positive only — no ratings, no comments, no downvotes
- Silence is meaningful — a profile with no vouches says everything
- Only verified nodes can write — kills bots and fake reviews immediately
- Open source — nobody owns this, it belongs to the communities that use it
- Trust as a commons — like water, like language, like whakapapa

## The meta-trust problem

You don't need to know Phil. You need to know that people who showed up for their community for decades know Phil. The vouching chain is the trust. It is whakapapa expressed in code.

## Origin

Vouch was conceived in Aotearoa New Zealand by Phil Kupenga (Ngāti Porou), founder of Next Chapter Consulting NZ, as part of a broader thesis that AI will come after our decisions — and that community trust networks are the defence.

The concept draws on Māori frameworks of whanaungatanga, manaakitanga, and whakapapa — not as cultural branding, but as commercially substantive differentiators in a world where relational capital is becoming scarce.
